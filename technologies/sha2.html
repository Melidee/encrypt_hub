RSA: Public-Key Cryptography

What is RSA Actually

RSA is a form of public-key cryptography theorized by cryptographers Ron Rivest, Adi Shamir, and Leonard Adleman, it is used to securely share information in scenarios that may be prone to interception from malicious attackers

How is RSA Used

RSA works through a system of public and private keys, for example, if you wanted to send a message in RSA you would encrypt it with the reciever's public key, then send it to them, and only them with their private key could decrypt it

What are the Parts of RSA

RSA is made up of many components, denoted by symbols and letters, E is the public key, it is most commonly 65537, D is the private key to decrypt the message, C is the ciphertext, or the number which represents the encrypted message, P & Q are large prime numbers used to generate the modulo, N is the modulo, a number known by both parties to facilitate the transaction

How does RSA Encryption Work

1) obtain N and E from the recipient
2) takes a number M as the message which is greater than one and less than the modulo
3) computes the ciphertext as ((M ^ E) mod N) (mod finds the remainder of the numbers)

How does RSA Decryption Work

1) Obtain the ciphertext C from the sender
2) Uses the known D to compute the message M as ((C ^ D) mod N)
3) The reciever now knows the message M



Number RSA Encryption

Generate ciphertext from M, N, E

Number RSA Decryption

Generate message from C, N, D

Plaintext RSA Encryption

Generate ciphertext from M, N, E

Plaintext RSA Decryption

Generate message from C, N, D
